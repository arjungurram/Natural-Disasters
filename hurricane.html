Hurricane

Hurricanes are tropical cyclones. In detail, they can also be reffered to as warm core storms. To form, hurricanes need warm oceans and moist air. As water vapor condenses, rising around the eye wall, heat is generated. It's normal for hurricanes to last 5-10 days. Winds push them until they're over cooler land or sea. When a hurricane disintigrates, it's replaced with intense rains and tornadoes. High wind shear can also disrupt the structure of a hurricane, eradicating it. It is also generally known that the right side of the hurricane is usually the most dangerous due to higher winds, waves, and storm surge. The right-front quadrant is a more detailed method of expression. This can apply for all hurricanes in the Northern Hemisphere. The Fujiwhara Effect happens when two tropical cyclones get close enough to each other to create a shared center, forcing the two storms to whip around that common central point. The stronger storm usually ends up absorbing the other one whether it's a tornado, or a hurricane.

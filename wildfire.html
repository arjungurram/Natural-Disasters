Wildfire

Wildfires are uncontrolled infernos that require three main components. These are a heat source, fuel, and oxygen. An example of a heat and fuel source would be the Sun and trees. About 89% of wildfires are caused by humans anually. Lightning is a frequent, natural cause of wildfires. These fires also surprisingly, typically move faster when traveling uphill. Wildfires move at an average of 14 miles per hour. Crown fires are spread by wind moving quickly across the tops of trees. Ground fires normally ignite in soil thick with organic matter that can feed the flames, like plant roots. They can go on for a long time, even an entire season. Surface fires are ignited by dry or dead vehetation just above the soil.

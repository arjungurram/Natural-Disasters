Landslide

Landslides are events when large amounts of sediment, dirt, sand, and sometimes gigantic boulders start rapidly flowing down a sloped surface. This can be classified as a form of mass movement. While this one is fast, other mass movements could be slow. Landslides usually have multiple causes rather than just one. Some examples include, rainfall, snowmelt, changes in water level, stream erosion, changes in ground water, earthquakes, volcanic activity, disturbance by human activities, or multiple of these factors put together. There are many types of landslides such as mudslides, rock falls, submarine landslides, solifluction, and deap-seated landslides. The main forms of movement these come from are slide, topple, spread, flow, and fall. The main types of material that these landslides are made of are rock, debris, soil, and bedrock.

Avalanche

An avalanche is a rapid flow of snow down a slope and is caused by either internal or external factors. Snowpack weakening is a form of internal affect. Some external effects are precipitation, animals, and earthquakes. There are four main types of avalanches. Loose Snow Avalanches are common on steep slopes and after fresh snowfall. These avalanches can in turn cause Slab Avalanches which are characterized as large blocks of ice moving down a slope. Thin slabs cause fairly small amounts of damage but the thick slabs cause many fatalities. Loose Snow and Slab avalanches can combine to create Powder Snow avalanches. The bottom half is a slab while the top half is a cloud of powdered snow that may grow exponentially over long distances. Wet Snow Avalanches are expecially dangerous as friction slows them down, allowing them to collect debris from the path fairly easily. While at the start, these avalanches are comprised of water and snow, further understanding of avalanches showed that speed can be picked up with ease.

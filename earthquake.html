Earthquake

An earthquake is the ground shaking caused by a sudden slip on a fault. Stresses in the earth's outer layer push the sides of the fault together. Stress builds up and the rocks slip suddenly, releasing energy in waves that travel through the earth's crust and cause the shaking that we feel during an earthquake. In simplified terms, earthquakes are the result of seismic waves in the lithosphere. The lithosphere is characterized to refer to all the land on earth. Earthquakes can last from seconds to decades. Aftershocks are lower magnitude earthquakes that occur after the main earthquake and normally number in the hundreds. These could also last for many years. Over time, the aftershocks grow less intense. An earthquakes magnitude is a way to express how powerful, intense, or dangerous it is.

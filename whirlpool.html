Whirlpool

A whirlpool is a body of rotating water produced by opposing currents or a current running into an obstacle. Smaller whirlpools can form when a sink is draining. Stronger ones formed in seas or oceans are often referred to as maelstroms. Most whirlpools aren't dangerous. The main danger is getting caught in a strong current. If you ever get caught in an undertow then swim parallel to the shoreline and let the undertow take you out until it subsides. Afterwards, swim back to the shore. Attempting to swim out of an undertow will only waste your energy, and in doing so, decrease the likelyhood of actually succeeding. Some whirlpools form and disappear over a short period of time. Others may be kept for centuries by water systems.

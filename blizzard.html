Blizzard

Blizzards are dangerous winter storms that are a combination of blowing snow, wind resulting in decreased visibility, and frigid temperatures. Falling snow actually isn't a requirement for a snowstorm to be classified as a blizzard. A blizzard that results from snow that has previously fallen is called a ground blizzard. Most blizzards occur between December and February. They also typically last at least three hours. The typical ground blizzard occurs when an Arctic cold front moves through the region, causing temperatures to drop and winds to increase quite rapidly, often reaching gusts of 50 to 60 mph. Blizzards can easily inflict people with hypothermia or frostbite. Usually they occur in temperate and polar regions at high mountains.

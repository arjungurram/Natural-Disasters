Volcano

A volcano is an opening in the earth's crust through which lava, volcanic ash, and gases escape. Volcanic eruptions are partly driven by pressure from dissolved gas. A volcanic eruption is the expulsion of gases, rock fragments, molten lava, or a combination of them from within the Earth through a vent onto the Earth's surface or into the atmosphere. Ash from volcanoes is also able to spark lightning. The volcanic crater, sometimes known as the caldera, is the bowl-shaped area at the top of the volcano where the vent at the top of the volcano which spews volcanic matter lies. While this is seen as a disaster sometimes, not all eruptions are bad. Volcanoes have created more than 80% of Earth's surface. Volcanoes are created when Earth's tectonic plates move together or apart, causing magma to come up to the earth's surface.

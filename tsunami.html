Tsunami

Tsunamis are huge waves as tall as buildings that wash over harbors and rush through cities, often leaving them in shambles. They may also be the aftermath of earthquakes that have taken place on the seafloor. As tsunamis near land, they grow exponentially and sometimes envelop entire streets. Other times, luckily, walls that have been specialized to maintain water levels and prevent haevy flooding manage to reduce or completely stop risk of flooding. Its not just underwater earthquakes that cause this widespread destruction. Just about any underwater explosion such as underwater volcanic eruptions or meteorite impacts can cause a once great city, to become a watery grave. After an incedent dating back to the 1950s, some had believed that large submarine landslides could be another cause of tsunamis. Some believe that large landslides from volcanic islands can trigger megatsunamis with the capability to cross oceans.

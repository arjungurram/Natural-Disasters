Tornado

Tornadoes are funnel shaped clouds moving in a downward spiral. They can develop from severe thunderstorms. Some conditions for a tornado to be created include warm moist air near the ground, cooler dry air above, unstable air, change of wind direction, and change of wind velocity. Upper level winds must be present or the tornado won't be formed. To take cover from an active tornado, seek shelter in a room in your home without windows. Don't seek shelter in big spaces like auditoriums that have large free-span roofs. Get out of your car if you're driving! A tornado can pick up a car more easily than it can pick up you crouched on the ground! If you are stuck in your house, don't go in a room with heavy items right above you whether they are in the room or on the floor directly above.

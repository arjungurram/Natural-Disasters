Asteroid

An asteroid is a minor planet that orbits within the inner Solar System. They have no atmosphere and can be either icy, rocky, or metallic. They're also known as rocky remnants left over from the early formation of our solar system. When asteroids collide with Earth, some effects include, shock waves, heat radiation, craters, earthquakes, and tsunamis if bodies of water are hit. This happens when the orbit of an asteroid overlaps with the orbit of the earth. A positive of asteroids is that they offer rare metals, minerals, and some organic compounds like water. About every 500,000 years there is a 1-kilometer asteroid that hits earth. A 10-kilometer asteroid unleashes enough energy to cause global catastrophe.

Sinkhole

A sinkhole is a depression or hole in the ground that has no natural external surface drainage. Basically, this means that when it rains, all of the water stays inside the sinkhole and typically drains deeper into the ground. They're caused by some form of collapse of the surface layer. Some causes include seasonal changes in the groundwater table, freeze and thaw of the ground, and extremes in precipitation. This could mean a drought or heavy rain. Water can drain through a sinkhole into an underground channel or a cave. Sinkholes are often funnel-shaped, with the wide end open at the surface and the narrow end at the bottom of the pool. Size can vary greatly. You may stumble across a small, shallow hole, or a large, deep pit.
